What is a Triangle

According to urban dictionary, a Triangle is a plane figure with three straight sides and three angles.

So does it mean that any combination of measurement and angles can create a triangle?

You guessed right, the answer is NO!



So what are the fundamental rules of a triangle ?


If any of the two rules are not met, then a triangle cannot be formed.

  • The three angles add up to 180 degrees
  • The sum of the two smaller side is greater than the largest side


Based on the measurement of the sides, triangles can be:

  • Equilateral(all three sides are equal)
  • Isosceles(two sides are equal)
  • Scalene(all three sides are different)


Based on measurements of their angles, triangles can be:

  • Acute(all angles are acute)
  • Right(one angle is right)
  • Obtuse(one angle is obtuse)
  • Equiangular(all angles are equal)
